# Corridor Reality Test — Facilitator Script

**Version**: 1 — FROZEN for five-pair collection run.
Do not revise scripted phrases, session structure, or the corridor concept question
until all five pairs are complete. Mid-run changes contaminate the aggregate.
To revise after the run: create Version 2 in a separate file; do not edit this one.

---

**Purpose**: Guide a test conductor through a vocabulary test for six connection-state labels.
This script is for the facilitator only. Do not share it with participants.

**Documents needed for a session**:
- This script (facilitator only)
- `CORRIDOR_PARTICIPANT_SHEET.md` — one copy per participant, held by facilitator and revealed in stages
- `CORRIDOR_SCORING_RUBRIC.md` — one copy per participant, completed by facilitator during session
- Pens or pencils for participants to write silent answers

---

## Pre-Session Checklist

- [ ] Two devices with a registered SCP identity, or a prepared slide/printout that can show each
      state label on cue (Active → Warm → Dormant → Active recovery)
- [ ] Scoring rubrics printed: one per participant (ten for a full five-pair run)
- [ ] Participant sheets printed and stapled so later sections cannot be seen before their step
- [ ] Pairs confirmed as people who actually know each other (the corridor model assumes prior trust)
- [ ] Physical space where both participants can write without seeing each other's paper

---

## Critical Discipline — Read Before Every Session

The purpose of this test is to find words that fail. Misunderstanding is the evidence you need.
If you correct participants, prompt the right answer, or confirm their guess, you are destroying
the data. The following behaviors are banned for the duration of the session:

**Banned phrases**:
- "You're right" / "Exactly" / "That's what we meant"
- "Actually, it means..."
- "Think of it like X" (any analogy)
- "Does that make sense?"
- "So you're saying the connection is [restatement that fixes the answer]?"

**When a participant asks "What does it mean?"**:
Say exactly: *"I want to hear what it means to you first. There are no wrong answers — we'll talk
through it all after we're done."*

**When a participant says "I think it means X — is that right?"**:
Say exactly: *"Just write down your first impression. We'll discuss everything at the end."*

**When a participant seems confused or stalls**:
Say exactly: *"Write whatever comes to mind. Even 'I don't know' is a useful answer."*

Do not react visibly to any answer — correct, confused, or surprising.

---

## Session Opening Script

Read this aloud to both participants together before separating them:

> "Thank you for being here. We're testing whether some status labels in a communication app
> communicate clearly without any explanation attached to them. There are no right or wrong
> answers — we're not testing you, we're testing the words. At a few points I'll ask you
> what you think a status means. Please write your answers independently without discussing
> them with each other. I'll collect answers before we talk about anything."

Do not describe the states or the system further. If participants ask what the app does:
say *"It's a secure communication app that keeps track of trusted connections between people
who know each other."*

---

## Part 1 — Establishing the Connection

**Facilitator action**: Have both participants complete the in-person connection setup on
their devices. Both parties must be physically present. This is a bilateral setup; one-sided
setup does not model the scenario being tested.

Once setup is complete, show each participant the connection screen showing **Active**.

**Reveal**: Uncover Section 1 of the participant sheet for each participant simultaneously.

**Wait**: Give both participants time to read the scenario and the question at the bottom
of Section 1. They write their answers silently and independently.

**Collect**: Physically collect both written answers before continuing. Record verbatim in
the scoring rubric under "Active — Pair N".

**Do not comment on the answers.**

---

## Part 2 — Transition to Warm

**Facilitator action**: Simulate time passage or a reduced exchange rate until the device
shows **Warm**. On a test device this may be done by advancing the clock or using the
simulator controls. The exact mechanism is not shown to participants — they see only that
the status has changed.

**Reveal**: Uncover Section 2 of the participant sheet.

**Wait**: Both participants write independently.

**Collect**: Collect both answers. Record verbatim under "Warm — Pair N".

**Do not comment on the answers.**

---

## Part 3 — Transition to Dormant

**Facilitator action**: Extend the quiet period until the device shows **Dormant**.

**Reveal**: Uncover Section 3 of the participant sheet.

**Wait**: Both participants write independently.

**Collect**: Collect both answers. Record verbatim under "Dormant — Pair N".

**Do not comment on the answers.**

---

## Part 4 — Recovery via Reaffirmation

**Facilitator action**: Prompt both participants to initiate a contact attempt from their
devices. Both parties must act — a unilateral message does not constitute reaffirmation.
Once the bilateral exchange completes and the status returns to **Active** (or **Warm**),
show participants the recovered state.

**Reveal**: Uncover Section 4 of the participant sheet.

**Wait**: Both participants write independently.

**Collect**: Collect both answers. Record verbatim under "Recovery — Pair N".

**Do not comment on the answers.**

---

## Part 5 — Vocabulary Review

This section tests the three states that may not have appeared naturally in the scenario
(Suspended, Severed, Burned) alongside the three already seen (Active, Warm, Dormant).
All six are tested here for completeness and to let participants revise earlier impressions
with fresh eyes.

**Reveal**: Uncover Section 5 of the participant sheet. This section shows each word in
a neutral status-display context and asks "What do you think this means for your connection?"

Both participants complete the vocabulary review silently. Do not read the words aloud
or vary your tone between them.

**Collect**: Collect both answer sheets. Record each response verbatim on the scoring rubric.

---

## Part 6 — Debrief (After All Answers Collected)

Only after all written answers are in your possession:

Explain the intended meaning of each state (refer to ARCHITECTURE_REALITY_GATE.md §2 for
the authoritative definitions). Let participants ask questions.

Record any vocabulary complaints, confusion, or proposed alternatives that surface during
debrief in the "Debrief notes" field on the scoring rubric.

Debrief observations are supplementary data, not part of the scored interpretation.

**Corridor concept comprehension — observe and record separately for each participant**:

These two questions are distinct from vocabulary scoring. Answer them from observation
across the whole session, not just the vocabulary review. Record your answer on the
rubric's "Corridor concept" block.

1. Did this participant understand that corridor establishment requires both parties to
   act, and that neither party can establish a corridor unilaterally?

2. Did this participant understand that a dormant or faded corridor can be recovered by
   bilateral action, and that recovery requires both parties — not just one reaching out?

A vocabulary failure is a label problem. A failure on either of these two questions is a
product-model problem. They require different responses.

Also record: did any participant frame a state as blame, punishment, or a judgment about
the other person's behaviour — separate from whether the word itself was understood?
(e.g., "I must have done something wrong", "they decided to punish me", "they betrayed me")

---

## After the Session

Transfer all verbatim responses to `CORRIDOR_TEST_RESULTS.md` under the appropriate pair block.
Assign Correct / Ambiguous / Incorrect per the criteria in `CORRIDOR_SCORING_RUBRIC.md`.
Do not score from memory; score from the written responses collected during the session.

Repeat for all five pairs before computing the aggregate table.

---

## Scheduling Note

Run one pair per session. Do not run two pairs simultaneously in the same room — participants
from different pairs must not hear each other's debrief. The debrief from Pair 1 must not
reach Pair 2 before Pair 2's session.
