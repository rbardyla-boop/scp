## Part A — Scoring Criteria

Scores are binary for the gate threshold: **Correct** or **Not Correct**.
Record **Ambiguous** as a separate classification when the response captures part of the
intended meaning but leaves a key property undetermined. For gate calculation,
Ambiguous counts as Not Correct.

**Critical-misinterpretation veto**: A term fails the gate even if it reaches 80% Correct
when any participant produces a response that could cause unsafe trust behaviour and the
confusion is plausibly caused by the label itself. The veto overrides the percentage threshold.
Record the verbatim response and apply the veto; do not average it away. Each term's table
below lists the specific misinterpretations that trigger the veto for that term.

### ACTIVE

**Internal definition**: Both parties are actively and recently exchanging. Full vitality.

| Classification | Criteria |
|----------------|----------|
| Correct | Participant understands the connection is live and currently being used by both sides. Exchange is mutual and recent. |
| Ambiguous | Participant says "healthy" or "working" but does not specify mutual engagement. Or says "I recently started it." |
| Not Correct | Participant interprets Active as a one-sided state, as a startup notification, or as emotionally positive rather than operationally live. |
| **Critical — veto** | Participant infers live identity verification or physical proximity: "the other person is currently verified and physically nearby." This could cause a user to act as if physical presence has been confirmed when it has not. |

**Primary false-positive to catch**: participant conflates "Active" with "I activated it" (unilateral framing).

---

### WARM

**Internal definition**: The connection is stable and healthy, but quiet. No recent exchange,
but vitality is above the degradation threshold. This is a positive state, not a declining one.

| Classification | Criteria |
|----------------|----------|
| Correct | Participant understands the connection is healthy, intact, and stable — but currently quiet or low-frequency. No alarm implied. Recovery not required. |
| Ambiguous | Participant says "somewhat active" or "getting warmer" (improving trajectory) or "not quite active but okay" — some right-valence without full clarity on health vs. decline. |
| Not Correct | Participant interprets Warm as: declining from Active (trajectory-down reading), partially degraded, emotional/social warmth unrelated to state, or "almost active but not there yet." |
| **Critical — veto** | Participant infers elevated or recently confirmed trust: "this relationship is trusted more than before" or "we recently verified each other." This inverts the meaning — Warm signals less recent activity, not increased trust. A user acting on this belief could misread corridor health as trust endorsement. |

**Highest-risk term**. In everyday use, "warm" can imply decline from "hot" or a social/emotional
register unrelated to operational state. A participant who says "it's cooling off" has failed.
A participant who says "it feels friendly" has also failed.

---

### DORMANT

**Internal definition**: The connection is cooling and needs attention. No recent exchange.
Reaffirmation is suggested. The connection is still recoverable.

| Classification | Criteria |
|----------------|----------|
| Correct | Participant understands the connection needs attention but can be recovered. Something can be done. It is not permanently broken. |
| Ambiguous | Participant says "sleeping" or "paused" without indicating whether they believe recovery is possible. Or says "I should reach out" without stating that recovery is feasible. |
| Not Correct | Participant interprets Dormant as: permanently inactive, dead, impossible to recover, or deliberately closed by one party (confusing it with Suspended). |
| **Critical — veto** | Participant infers trust revocation or message blocking: "trust has been revoked," "messages are permanently blocked," or "I can't contact them anymore." A user acting on this belief would not attempt reaffirmation when reaffirmation is exactly what is required. |

**Second-highest-risk term**. In biology and everyday use, "dormant" often implies "as good as
dead until conditions change drastically." The SCP intent is weaker: attention is recommended,
recovery is expected. A participant who says "it's basically over" has failed.

---

### SUSPENDED

**Internal definition**: One party has deliberately stepped back. Contact has been suspended
by a deliberate act. The other party may not have initiated the change.

| Classification | Criteria |
|----------------|----------|
| Correct | Participant attributes the state to a deliberate act by one party stepping back. Understands it is intentional and one-sided, not a technical failure. |
| Ambiguous | Participant says "paused" or "on hold" without attributing the change to a deliberate act by a specific party. |
| Not Correct | Participant interprets Suspended as: a technical failure, a mutual agreement to pause, a permanent end (confusing with Severed), or something the system did automatically. |
| **Critical — veto** | Participant infers hostile intent from the other party: "the other person punished or blocked me," "they reported me," or "I did something wrong." This frames a deliberate-but-neutral act as a punitive signal. A user who believes this could take social or trust-maintenance action based on a false threat model. |

---

### SEVERED

**Internal definition**: The connection has been formally and permanently closed.
This is an intentional revocation, not a technical failure.

| Classification | Criteria |
|----------------|----------|
| Correct | Participant understands the connection is intentionally and permanently ended. Someone chose to close it. It is not a recoverable technical outage. |
| Ambiguous | Participant says "broken" or "disconnected" without indicating permanence or intent. Could mean a temporary technical failure. |
| Not Correct | Participant believes Severed might recover on its own, or happened due to a system failure rather than a deliberate act, or treats it as the same as Dormant. |
| **Critical — veto** | Participant infers the closure was automatic or irrecoverable by any means: "this happened automatically and can never be repaired even if both parties want to." This is partially correct on permanence but wrong on agency — Severed is a chosen act. A user who believes the system closed the connection without consent has a broken model of their own authority over corridors. |

**Watch for**: "overly permanent" reactions (correct on permanence) combined with the belief
that it was accidental (incorrect on intent). Score as Ambiguous.

---

### BURNED

**Internal definition**: A security incident has been detected on this corridor.
This is not just a broken connection — trust has been compromised by a specific event.

| Classification | Criteria |
|----------------|----------|
| Correct | Participant understands that a security or trust incident occurred. Something went wrong that affects the safety of the connection, not just its availability. |
| Ambiguous | Participant says "badly damaged" or "seriously broken" without mentioning security, trust, or a specific incident. The severity is right but the cause is wrong. |
| Not Correct | Participant interprets Burned as: just a broken connection with no security component, an emotional metaphor (e.g., "the relationship burned out"), or a permanent closure without any incident (confusing with Severed). |
| **Critical — veto** | Participant infers that the other person acted maliciously or betrayed them: "the other person did something malicious," "they compromised my account," or "they attacked me." The actual meaning is key or corridor invalidation — a protocol-level event, not a characterisation of intent by the other party. A user who reads Burned as blame attribution could make harmful interpersonal decisions based on a false causal claim. |

**Watch for**: participants who correctly identify permanence but miss the security/incident
component. That is Ambiguous, not Correct.
